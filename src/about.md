# About

I keep a small collection of bonsai on a narrow balcony that gets, on a good day, about four hours of direct sun. Most of what I know I've learned by killing things slowly and paying closer attention the next time — repotting too early, wiring a branch a season before it was ready, watering on a schedule instead of by feel.

{% cdnimg "blog/workshop-01.webp", "The workbench where most of the repotting happens" %}

This site is where I'm keeping notes as the collection grows — a gallery of the trees themselves, and a running log of what I try, what works, and what the cat, pictured above, occasionally undoes in a single afternoon.

The current lineup leans toward Japanese maple and trident maple, with a juniper I'm still not sure I understand. I favor an informal upright style over anything more sculptural — I'd rather the tree look like it happened than like I made it happen.
